Our Legacy

Decades of tradition, passion, and excellence on the water

The Story of Shadwell Dragons

Dragon boating has ancient roots, stretching back over 2,000 years, but our team’s journey is firmly rooted in East London. In 1995, Shadwell Dragons was founded by a group of young Bengali men from Shadwell who shared a passion for sport and community. The team was originally known as Shadwell Solander before proudly adopting the name Shadwell Dragons, reflecting its deep connection to the local area.

Dragon Boating Comes to London

The sport arrived in the UK during the 1980s and quickly gained popularity. By the early 1990s, clubs were forming across the country. With its historic waterways and vibrant, multicultural communities, London became a natural home for dragon boating.

Birth of Shadwell Dragons

In 1995, that spirit took hold in Shadwell. What began as a small initiative by local young men soon grew into something much larger. Training out of the historic Shadwell Basin, once a busy Victorian dock and now a striking waterside setting with views of Canary Wharf, the team built a strong foundation rooted in friendship, determination, and community pride.

From its early days as Shadwell Solander to becoming Shadwell Dragons, the club has grown from a single crew into one of London’s most established dragon boat teams, known for its competitive drive and its strong community ethos.

Honoring Our Past, Building Our Future

We're proud of our history, but we're even more excited about our future. Every new member adds to our legacy. Every race, every training session, every laugh shared on the water becomes part of our story.
