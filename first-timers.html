First Timers Welcome

Your Journey Starts Here

Dragon boating might look intimidating, but it's one of the most accessible water sports out there. You don't need to be super fit, you don't need experience, and you definitely don't need to know how to swim (though it helps!). What you do need is enthusiasm and a willingness to try something new.

What is Dragon Boating?

Dragon boating is a team paddling sport where 20 paddlers sit side-by-side in pairs, working together to power a 40-foot decorated boat through the water. A drummer sits at the front keeping rhythm, and a steersperson at the back keeps the boat on course. It's exhilarating, social, and an incredible workout.

Your First Session

We offer free taster sessions every week where you can try dragon boating with no commitment. Here's what happens:

  • Welcome & Safety Briefing (15 mins) - Meet the team, learn about equipment, and understand basic safety
  • On-Land Technique (20 mins) - Learn the paddling stroke on dry land before getting in the boat
  • On-Water Practice (60 mins) - Get in the boat and experience the thrill of paddling together
  • Debrief & Questions (15 mins) - Chat about joining the team, ask any questions

What to Bring

  • Clothes you don't mind getting wet (sports gear works great)
  • Change of clothes for afterwards
  • Water bottle
  • Towel
  • Sun protection in summer
  • Positive attitude!

Common Questions

Do I need to be fit?

Not at all! Dragon boating is suitable for all fitness levels. You'll get fitter as you go, and you control your own intensity.

What if I can't swim?

All paddlers wear life jackets, and we have safety procedures in place. However, we do recommend learning to swim for your own confidence.

What age do I need to be?

We welcome paddlers from age 12 upwards. Anyone under 18 needs parental consent.

How much does it cost?

Taster sessions are completely free! If you decide to join, check our pricing page for membership options.
